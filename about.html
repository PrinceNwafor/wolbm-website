Our Story

About Word of Life
Bible Ministry

A prophetic ministry born out of prayer, built on the Word, and sustained by the Holy Spirit since 2004.

Our Mission

Connecting Men to God
and Their Purpose

Word of Life Bible Ministry (WOLBM) exists to make fully devoted and fully developed disciples of Jesus Christ. We are called to reach the lost with the gospel, to heal the broken, and to empower every believer to walk in the fullness of God's purpose for their life.

Our ministry is especially focused on those society has overlooked — the castoffs, the struggling, the forgotten. We believe every life has immeasurable worth in God's eyes, and we are committed to meeting people exactly where they are.

Through prophetic ministry, the preached Word, healing services, and discipleship, WOLBM has been transforming lives in South Florida and beyond since 2004.

How It All Began

A Ministry Born
in a Living Room

1

The Divine Call (2004)

Prophet Dr. Ige Kelly Osaigbovo received a divine mandate from God in 2004. Responding in obedience, he began gathering a small prayer group in his living room — a modest beginning that would grow into a mighty move of God.

2

Growth Through the Word

As the anointing grew, so did the congregation. The ministry expanded beyond the living room into a full church, marked by powerful preaching, prophetic declarations, signs, and wonders that drew people hungry for an authentic encounter with God.

3

Establishing in Pembroke Pines

WOLBM established its home at 233 N University Drive, Pembroke Pines, FL — becoming a lighthouse in South Florida. Sunday services, the Tuesday "Power Must Change Hands" revival meetings, and a radio program began reaching thousands across the region.

4

A Ministry Without Borders

Today, WOLBM's impact extends across nations through online services, a global prayer network, and outreach missions. The ministry continues to grow, powered by the same Holy Spirit fire that sparked it all in 2004.

Our Senior Pastor

Prophet Dr. Ige Kelly Osaigbovo

Prophet Dr. Ige Kelly Osaigbovo
Founder & Senior Pastor
Evangelist · Teacher · Prophet

Prophet Dr. Ige Kelly Osaigbovo is a man marked by God — an Evangelist, Teacher, and Prophet who carries a powerful anointing for healing, deliverance, and prophetic ministry.

Called into ministry in 2004, Prophet Ige Kelly began with a simple prayer group and a burning passion to reach those who felt overlooked by society and religion. His message has always been clear and uncompromising: God has a word for you, a plan for you, and power available for you — regardless of your past or your present circumstances.

His ministry is characterized by word-proven prophecy, supernatural healings, and the kind of authentic, spirit-filled preaching that cuts through religion and touches the human heart. Thousands have received prophetic words that redirected their lives, experienced physical healing in services, and been set free through the ministry of deliverance.

Beyond the pulpit, Prophet Ige Kelly is known for his compassion for the poor and marginalized. The ministry's outreach programs — including prison ministry, care ministry, and community support — reflect his personal conviction that the gospel must be demonstrated, not merely declared.

Under his leadership, WOLBM has grown from a small prayer group into a thriving, multi-ministry church serving South Florida and impacting nations through media and missions.

His Message

"Your Destiny Is More Than
What You Can See Right Now"

Prophet Ige Kelly's hallmark message is one of divine purpose and supernatural possibility. He consistently preaches that God's plans for His people far exceed their natural circumstances — their bank accounts, their failures, their limitations.

The Tuesday "Power Must Change Hands" revival meetings have become a weekly appointment for hundreds who come expecting a word from God and leave with a testimony. The radio program of the same name extends this ministry even further.

If you have never experienced a service at WOLBM, we invite you to come — not just to attend church, but to encounter the living God through His Word and Spirit.
