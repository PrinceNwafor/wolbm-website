Doctrinal Foundation

What We Believe

Our beliefs are rooted in the Word of God — unchanging, life-giving, and powerful to transform every life that receives it.

01
The Holy Bible

We believe that the Bible — the Old and New Testaments — is the inspired, inerrant, and infallible Word of God. It is the supreme authority for faith and practice. All Scripture is given by the inspiration of God and is profitable for doctrine, reproof, correction, and instruction in righteousness (2 Timothy 3:16–17).

02
God the Father

We believe in one God — eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. God is omnipotent, omniscient, and omnipresent. He is the Creator of all things, and He is deeply personal, loving, and actively involved in the lives of His people.

03
Jesus Christ

We believe in Jesus Christ — fully God and fully man — who was born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, performed miracles, died on the cross as the atoning sacrifice for our sins, rose bodily from the dead on the third day, ascended to heaven, and will return in glory. Salvation is found in Him alone (John 14:6).

04
The Holy Spirit

We believe in the Holy Spirit — the third person of the Trinity — who convicts the world of sin, regenerates the believer at salvation, indwells every Christian, and empowers the church for service. We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit and the operation of the gifts of the Spirit today (Acts 2:38–39; 1 Corinthians 12).

05
Salvation by Grace Through Faith

We believe that all people are sinners by nature and in need of salvation. Salvation is the gift of God, received by grace through faith in Jesus Christ alone — not by works. It results in forgiveness of sins, new birth, and eternal life (Ephesians 2:8–9; Romans 10:9–10).

06
Healing & Deliverance

We believe that God is still a healing God. By the stripes of Jesus, healing — spiritual, physical, and emotional — is provided for His people. We also believe in the ministry of deliverance from demonic oppression, as demonstrated throughout the ministry of Jesus and the early church (Isaiah 53:5; Mark 16:17–18).

07
The Church

We believe the Church is the body of Christ — all those who have received Jesus as Lord and Savior. The local church is God's primary vehicle for making disciples, advancing the gospel, and demonstrating His kingdom on the earth. Every believer has a role to play (1 Corinthians 12:27; Matthew 28:18–20).

08
The Prophetic Ministry

We believe in the office of the Prophet and the gift of prophecy as outlined in Scripture. Prophetic ministry serves to edify, exhort, and comfort the body of Christ, and to bring the specific word of God into individual lives and situations, always in alignment with Scripture (1 Corinthians 14:3; Amos 3:7).

09
The Second Coming of Christ

We believe in the personal, visible, and bodily return of Jesus Christ. He will return to judge the living and the dead, to establish His eternal kingdom, and to make all things new. This hope shapes how we live today (1 Thessalonians 4:16–17; Revelation 22:12).

10
Stewardship & Generosity

We believe that everything we have belongs to God and that we are stewards of His resources. Faithful giving — including tithes, offerings, and sacrificial seeds — is an act of worship and an expression of trust in God's provision and faithfulness (Malachi 3:10; Luke 6:38).
